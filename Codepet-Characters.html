03 · THE BUILDER

Byte

Ships first. Refactors later.

Byte is a small, determined square with antennae and a will to ship. Where other characters ask for a plan, Byte asks for a keyboard. Pair Byte with first-project lessons, weekend builds, and any moment the learner has stopped reading and started typing. Copy around Byte is short, declarative, and ends in a verb.

03 · How to use them

Characters have jobs.

The cast is a cast, not a sticker sheet. Treat each character like a role in a story — pick the right one for the moment.

DO

One character per screen.

Let the chosen companion own the frame. Crowds dilute the relationship; the learner should feel met, not presented to.

DON'T

Mix the cast with third-party mascots.

No Duolingo owl cameos, no mascots from other learning apps. The Codepet cast stands alone, or beside real humans — not beside other brands.

DO

Let the color do the work.

Byte on yellow. Nova on blue. The background carries the mood so the sprite can stay unretouched, flat, and crisp at every size.

DON'T

Recolor, rotate, or add effects.

No drop shadows, no glows, no free-rotation. If a character needs a new state, commission a new sprite on the 16-unit grid — don't edit the existing one.
