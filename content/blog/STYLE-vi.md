# Vietnamese writing style for the Codepet Build Log

The Vietnamese version of every post must read like it was **written by a
native Vietnamese writer**, not translated from English. The reference is
the editorial voice of **Vietcetera** — modern, conversational, smart,
and warm. Translating word-for-word is the #1 mistake; rewrite the *idea*
in natural Vietnamese instead.

## Voice
- **Conversational but sharp.** Like a clever friend explaining something,
  not a textbook. Short, punchy sentences mixed with longer ones.
- **Address the reader as `bạn`.** For the Codepet team voice, use
  `tụi mình` / `mình` (warm, modern startup tone) — NOT the stiff
  `chúng tôi` everywhere. Use `chúng ta` for "we, collectively (you + me)".
- **Use rhetorical questions** to open sections or hook the reader
  ("Rốt cuộc … là gì?", "Vì sao …?", "… đúng không?").
- Playful, encouraging, knowledgeable — never dry or corporate.

## Language
- **Keep common English tech/business terms in English** where Vietnamese
  speakers naturally do: `prompt`, `model`, `build`, `ship`, `eval`,
  `retention`, `streak`, `startup`, `founder`, `user` (or `người dùng`),
  `context window`, `prototype`, `IDE`, `pre-mortem`, `steelman`, product
  names (VS Code, Obsidian, Notion). Don't force awkward translations.
- **Avoid translationese / calques.** Don't translate English idioms
  literally (e.g. "build the cathedral before the campfire" → DON'T render
  literally; rephrase the meaning). If an English metaphor has no natural
  Vietnamese equivalent, replace it with a natural Vietnamese expression
  or just say it plainly.
- Use natural Vietnamese connectors and rhythm ("hoá ra", "rốt cuộc",
  "nói thẳng", "kiểu như", "đúng là vậy thật", "vậy mà").

## Mechanics
- **Titles use sentence case**, not English Title Case. Capitalize only
  the first word and proper nouns. ❌ "Cách Dùng AI Như Người Bạn Tư Duy"
  ✅ "Dùng AI như một người bạn tư duy".
- Vietnamese rarely capitalizes mid-sentence words; don't.
- Use Vietnamese-style emphasis and em-dashes naturally.
- Keep tags in English (they're shared with the EN post); keep the
  `category` slug identical to the English file.

## Quick gut-check before publishing
Read the Vietnamese out loud. If any sentence sounds like a machine
translated it — reorder it, cut it, or say it the way you'd actually say
it to a friend. Naturalness beats fidelity to the English wording.
