# Vietnamese writing style for the Codepet Build Log

The Vietnamese version of every post must read like a skilled Vietnamese
writer wrote it from scratch — **not** a translation of the English. The
reference is the editorial prose of **Vietcetera**: intelligent,
essayistic, warm, and polished. Word-for-word translation is the #1
failure mode. Re-think each idea in Vietnamese and write it the way you'd
write it *if the English didn't exist*.

## The voice (study real Vietcetera articles)
It is **magazine-quality essay**, not casual chat and not academic. Some
real openers to feel the register:

> "Có lẽ bởi cách bộ não chúng ta vận hành, mà có những ký ức tưởng chừng
> đã lãng quên từ rất lâu rồi, nhưng chỉ cần một chất xúc tác đầy ngẫu
> nhiên thôi - một âm thanh, một vài nốt nhạc, một mùi hương - chúng lại
> lập tức xuất hiện…"

> "Lừa đảo không còn mang dáng dấp của một phi vụ riêng lẻ, nó đã trở
> thành một hệ sinh thái vận hành như một ngành công nghiệp toàn cầu…"

Notice: **strong declarative openers**, long layered sentences that still
flow, vivid concrete imagery, and an intelligent reflective tone.

## Rules
- **Sentence craft is everything.** Write long, well-built sentences that
  flow, with clauses joined by commas and **spaced em-dashes ` - `**; mix
  in short punchy sentences for emphasis. Never choppy, never English word
  order. Read it aloud — it must sound like natural spoken-then-polished
  Vietnamese.
- **Pronouns:** `chúng ta` for shared truths (includes the reader); `bạn`
  to address the reader; `chúng tôi` for the Codepet team/story (measured,
  natural — Vietnamese often drops the subject entirely, do that too). Do
  NOT use slangy `tụi mình`.
- **Vocabulary:** vivid, precise, idiomatic. Use natural Vietnamese
  connectors and turns of phrase: *hoá ra, thật ra, suy cho cùng, đáng nói
  là, nói cho công bằng, chính xác hơn, vấn đề là, điều thú vị là*. Avoid
  flat literal renderings.
- **Loaded / coined / emphatic terms go in "quotes"**; titles & foreign
  terms in *italics*; **bold** a key turn of thought occasionally.
- **Rhetorical questions** to open or pivot a section.
- **Keep common English tech/business terms in English**: prompt, model,
  build, ship, eval, retention, streak, startup, founder, user, context
  window, RAG, product names (VS Code, Obsidian, Notion).
- **No literal calques of English idioms.** If an English metaphor has no
  natural Vietnamese equivalent, replace it with a Vietnamese one or say
  it plainly.
- **Titles in sentence case**, not English Title Case. Capitalize only the
  first word and proper nouns.
- Keep `tags` in English; keep the `category` slug identical to the EN
  file; same slug.

## Final gut-check
Read every Vietnamese paragraph out loud. If a single sentence sounds like
a machine produced it — reorder it, change the vocabulary, or rewrite it
the way you'd actually say it. Naturalness and flow beat fidelity to the
English wording, every time.
