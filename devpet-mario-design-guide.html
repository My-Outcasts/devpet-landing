DevPet × Nintendo

Character Design Guideline · v0.1 · 2026-04-20

1 · Design Philosophy

DevPet's visual identity borrows from Super Mario Bros. (SMB1, NES 1985): hardware-constrained, icon-first pixel art where silhouette does most of the work. Every design decision flows from one principle:

If a player can't identify the character from its silhouette alone at 16×16, it's not done.

The NES's technical limits are not restrictions we work around — they are the style. Embrace them.

2 · Canvas & Grid

2.1 Sprite Size

PropertyValueNote
Canvas16 × 16 pxMatches NES small-Mario, Goomba, Super Star
Pixel aspect1:1 squareNo rectangular pixels
Render scale2× / 3× / 4× nearest-neighborInteger multiples only
Grid originTop-left (0, 0)Column 0 = leftmost, row 0 = top

2.2 Figure Proportions

Characters occupy roughly 10–12 columns wide × 12–14 rows tall within the 16×16 canvas, with at least 2 px of margin on the sides and 1–2 px top/bottom.

2.3 Symmetry Rule

Default to vertical symmetry (axis at column 7.5 / 8). Break symmetry only when personality demands it — never for decoration.

3.2 Per-Sprite Color Budget

  • Max 4 colors per sprite (transparent counts as color 0), mirroring NES PPU constraints
  • One should be a darker shade of the dominant color for depth
  • One may be a contrast accent (white or black)

3.3 Forbidden

  • Gradients or anti-aliased edges
  • Pure mid-gray #808080 (muddies the palette)
  • Any color not in the master palette above

4 · Silhouette Rules

A silhouette should tell you what kind of thing the character is before you see any face.

Shape LanguageReads As
Wider than tall, rounded ears on topBear / mushroom helper
Two triangular peaks on topCat / fox / animal
Dome top with hat brim lineHuman / plumber
Wavy / rounded blobGhost / slime
Radial pointsStar / sparkle / item
Arched body with small feetGoomba / villain

Always draw the silhouette first as solid black on sky blue. If it doesn't read, adjust before adding color.

5 · Face & Expression System

5.1 Eye Conventions

StyleFootprintUsed ByReads As
Vertical bar1 × 2HeroFocused, determined
Solid block2 × 2Mentor, VillainStandard expressive
Block + white bg2 × 3Power-Up, VillainCartoon, emotional

5.2 Mouth Conventions

  • Line of 2–4 dark pixels = neutral / determined
  • Downward curve of 3 pixels = worried
  • Upward curve of 3 pixels = happy
  • Open square with fangs inside = angry (Villain)

5.3 Expression Principle

At 16×16 a single pixel changes the entire mood. Budget one expression slot per sprite — eyes OR mouth, rarely both.

6 · The Four Archetypes

01
HERO — Coder Plumber

Mario Main companion. Your constant presence in the app.

SilhouetteHat dome → face → torso → separated legs
Color budgetPurple, purple-dark, peach, brown + cyan accent
Key featuresPurple hat, brown hair peek, mustache, cyan code-glow chest patch, brown boots
Eye styleSingle visible eye (NES-authentic asymmetry)
Expression slotDetermined / focused

DO: Keep the hat + mustache recognizable even stripped of color.
DON'T: Give the hero a modern logo on the hat — one cyan dot at most.

02
MENTOR — Mushroom Helper

Toad Wise guide. Appears during lessons and deep dives.

SilhouetteWide mushroom dome → narrow neck → small body with arm stubs
Color budgetTeal, teal-dark, white, peach + black
Key featuresTeal cap with 4 white spots, big 2×2 eyes, tiny mouth, small white body
Eye style2×2 solid black blocks
Expression slotNeutral warmth

DO: Keep the cap-to-body width ratio at least 2:1 — Toad's readability depends on it.
DON'T: Give the Mentor a human face. The charm is the "mushroom with eyes" illusion.

03
POWER-UP — Ship-It Star

Super Star Celebration. Triggers on every ship, streak, level-up.

Silhouette5-pointed star, radial
Color budgetYellow, yellow-dark, black + white
Key featuresTwo eyes, mouth, shadow on lower points for depth
Eye style2×2 black on yellow
Expression slotHappy / invincible

DO: Use yellow-dark on the lower three points — gives the star its "chunky" NES feel.
DON'T: Add sparkle pixels inside the sprite. Sparkles are animation, not geometry.

04
VILLAIN — Glitch Goomba

Goomba Antagonist. Appears on errors, abandoned streaks, Null Cascade events.

SilhouetteRounded dome body with narrow feet
Color budgetPurple-dark, purple-deep, white, black + gray feet
Key featuresV eyebrows, angry sideways-looking pupils, open fanged mouth
Eye style2×3 white with 1×1 black pupil offset outward
Expression slotAngry / threatening

DO: Keep feet visually separate from body (1 px gap) — reads as "walking enemy" instantly.
DON'T: Make the Villain use primary purple. Deep dark variant signals "not a friend."

7 · Composition & Environment

7.1 Background

  • Default: sky (#5C94FC)
  • No gradients
  • Ground line (when needed): 2 px horizontal band of #663C00 at the bottom

7.2 Cast Line-Up

  • Shared baseline at row 15
  • 4 pixels of sky between sprites horizontally
  • Sprites never overlap (NES couldn't, we don't)

7.3 Scale Variants

Use caseRender scale
Inline icon in text2× (32×32)
Roster card3× (48×48)
Hero stage / onboarding4× (64×64)
Animated showcase6–8× (96–128)

Always image-rendering: pixelated in CSS or shape-rendering: crispEdges in SVG.

8 · Animation

8.1 Frame Budget

Each character: 2–3 frames max. Matches SMB1 walk cycles.

8.2 Per-Character Idle Motion

CharacterIdleFramesTiming
HeroShallow breath bob2500 ms/frame
MentorSlight head tilt2700 ms/frame
Power-UpSpin wobble3150 ms/frame
VillainAngry twitch2600 ms/frame

8.3 Motion Rules

  • Integer pixel translation only — no sub-pixel easing
  • Vertical bob: 1 px max
  • Rotation: forbidden (draw a new frame instead)
  • Scale: forbidden (same reason)

8.4 Event Animations

  • Ship-it: Power-Up pops from screen center, 3 sparkle frames, exits up
  • Error: Villain slides in from right, stays 1 s, slides out left
  • Level-up: Hero 4-frame jump (up 2, up 4, down 2, down 0)

9 · Typography Pairing

  • Headlines: Press Start 2P (Google Fonts) — closest to NES nameplate lettering
  • Letter spacing: 2px
  • Color on sky: #000, with 2px 2px 0 #FFF drop shadow for readability
  • Body text: standard monospace (Courier New, ui-monospace)

11 · Asset Data Format

Sprites authored as string grids for easy editing:

const CHAR = {
  grid: [
    '................',
    '....PPPPPP......',
    // ... 16 rows total, 16 chars per row
  ],
  palette: {
    '.': null,           // transparent
    'P': '#7F1EDF',      // purple
    'p': '#4B0F8A',      // purple-dark
    // ...
  },
  meta: {
    name: 'HERO',
    baselineRow: 15,
    symmetric: false,
    eyeStyle: '1x2 vertical',
  }
}

Renderer reads grid + palette and emits SVG <rect> elements at target scale.

12 · Dos & Don'ts Summary

DO

  • Keep sprites at 16×16
  • Use ≤4 colors per sprite
  • Default to vertical symmetry
  • Test silhouettes in solid black first
  • Animate with integer pixels only
  • Pair with Press Start 2P at UI

DON'T

  • Use gradients, anti-aliasing, or sub-pixel rendering
  • Add modern flourishes (soft shadows, glows)
  • Exceed 4 colors or break the master palette
  • Rotate or scale sprites in motion
  • Overlap sprites in composition
