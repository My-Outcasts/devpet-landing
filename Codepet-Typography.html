Role

Headlines & highlights

Use Minecraft when a word needs to stop the eye — section numbers, eyebrow labels, hero headlines, and the biggest calls to action.

Sizes

Small or huge, never middle

Micro (10–14 pt) for eyebrows and meta. Large (40 pt and up) for headlines and posters. Avoid the 18–32 pt middle zone — it reads muddy.

Rendering

Pixelated, always

image-rendering: pixelated on the web. .interpolation(.none) in SwiftUI. Never anti-alias a pixel-art face — it destroys the grid.

Role

Titles & long-form

PP Neue Montreal holds every surface where a reader slows down — H1–H3, paragraphs, menus, tooltips, captions, data labels.

Line length

Between 50 and 80 characters

For running text, aim for 60 characters per line. Tight grids can compress to 50. Never let a body paragraph stretch past 80.

Line height

1.5 for body, 1.1 for display

Body runs at 1.5. Pull-quotes and titles tighten to 1.1 so letters lock against each other. Never use the default 1.2 browser value.

How they work together.

CHAPTER 03 · LEVEL UP

YOUR FIRST SHIPPED APP

This week, you'll turn an idea in your head into a running product in your dock — shipped, opened, and used by someone other than you.

You will build in small, deliberate loops. Pick the smallest version of the thing, get it running, hand it to a friend, learn what broke. Every Codepet lesson is a rehearsal for the real motion: write, run, watch, repair. By Friday, the product will be real enough to install.

Eyebrow & title: Minecraft · Lede & body: PP Neue Montreal

The pair follows a rule: Minecraft announces, PP Neue explains. When you're tempted to set a paragraph in Minecraft — don't. When you're tempted to set a hero in PP Neue — pause and ask whether this moment should feel like a game or a document.

05
The rules

Do & don't.

Short list. Keep the pair clean, and every surface reads as Codepet on first glance.

Do
  • Use Minecraft sparingly. One word, one phrase, one eyebrow per surface. It is a spotlight, not a spotlight on everything.
  • Set it in uppercase or short words. Minecraft reads best in three to five characters at a time.
  • Use PP Neue Montreal for everything a reader scans or reads. Titles, paragraphs, UI, data.
  • Render Minecraft pixel-perfect. Integer scales only. Never bilinear, bicubic, or blurred.
  • Pair it with whitespace. Minecraft works loud — let the margins be loud too.
Don't
  • Don't set body text in Minecraft. It becomes unreadable past two lines. Always swap to PP Neue for anything longer than a phrase.
  • Don't size Minecraft between 18 and 32 pt. It falls into an awkward middle. Go smaller, or go much larger.
  • Don't anti-alias Minecraft. Smoothing kills the pixel grid and makes it look like a knockoff.
  • Don't mix italics. Minecraft doesn't do italic. Never synthesize one.
  • Don't introduce a third font. Two is the whole system. Never smuggle in a display script or a serif.
